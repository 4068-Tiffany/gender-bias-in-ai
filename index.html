01 — The Problem

AI systems are not
neutral mirrors

"Bias starts at data collection — not just in the algorithm."

Despite significant progress in AI capabilities, gender bias remains a persistent and measurable problem across AI systems. Facial recognition models perform significantly worse on women — particularly dark-skinned women. Job recommendation systems associate high-paying professions with men and lower-paying ones with women. Image generators consistently underrepresent women in positions of power.

These are not minor technical glitches — they are systemic patterns with real consequences: women being misidentified by security systems, steered away from career opportunities, and rendered invisible in the visual language of authority and expertise.

This project investigates, measures, and communicates these biases using real public datasets and rigorous statistical methods.

03 — Cross-Cutting Themes

Why this
matters

01

Bias starts in data, not algorithms

All three systems inherit their bias from training data that reflects historical inequality. The algorithm is often neutral but what it learns from is not.

02

Feedback loops amplify inequality

Biased AI outputs become tomorrow's training data. If job recommenders steer women away from senior roles, future datasets show fewer women there confirming the bias in a closed loop.

03

Intersectionality is the blind spot

Dark-skinned women face compounded bias. Most fairness tools still don't measure this intersection they evaluate gender or race, but not both simultaneously.

04

Measurement is the first act of justice

None of these biases can be fixed without first being quantified. This is why bias auditing datasets are not just academic exercises they are infrastructure for fairness.

04 — Recommendations

What should
change

For Practitioners

Audit training data intersectionally

Measure gender × race × age representation before model training. Overall accuracy hides who the system fails.

For Researchers

Build intersectional benchmarks

Single-axis fairness metrics are insufficient. Develop benchmarks that measure bias at the intersection of multiple identity dimensions.

For Organisations

Diversify AI teams

Lived experience catches what p-values miss. Include diverse voices at every stage — from data collection to deployment decisions.

For Everyone

Demand transparency

Ask AI vendors for disaggregated performance metrics. Accountability starts with visibility.
